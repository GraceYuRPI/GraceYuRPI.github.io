Rensselaer Motorsport

Rensselaer Motorsport is a club associated with the Mechanical, Aerospace, and Nuclear Engineering Department of RPI. We are a fourth-year FSAE Electric team. Last year we finished 7th at the Formula Hybrid + Electric Competition in New Hampshire and attended the Formula SAE Electric Competition in Michigan. We managed to get our first running car before competition last year and tested it extensively.
This is my third year on the team, I am the Senior Chassis-Suspension-Tires Lead. My primary responsibilities are designing the chassis and suspension systems, as well as conducting tires studies using various tire models and data to select the optimal tire and subsequently determine the suspension setup - the ultimate goal of my subsystem. In addition, as the senior lead, I am also responsible for teaching junior members of the team mechanical design and vehicle dynamics. I was Chassis-Suspension-Tires Lead last year and Chassis-Suspension Manufacturing Lead the year prior.
For RM28, the primary goal was to package everything into a robust rolling chassis as soon as possible, because we did not have a functional electrical system yet and could not validate many of our design decisions. However, this year, using last year's testing data, we were able to select many areas of the car to improve. However, for RM29, I had more leeway to optimize parts through lightweighting and DFM - such that not only the resultant parts are lighter, they are also easier to machine.

Projects

Tire Study

We design and build electric racecars on an annual basis. I am the current Chassis-Suspension-Tires Lead, working primarily on the vehicle dynamics side of the team.

Suspension Optimization

As part of a student team, we went to Canadian Light Source, a synchrotron particle accelerator, to investigate the effect of radiation damage at different temperatures, during X-ray diffraction experiments.

Parametric Chassis FEA and Optimization

This was a collaborative effort between a friend and I, where we investigated the effectiveness of generatively designing airfoil geometries using ML algorithms. It was a continuation of a passion project that I did back in Grade 10 on using data augmentation techniques to improve the design selection process of airfoils with single row vertex generators. This new version aimed to focus purely on airfoils to improve data validity and investigate the best suited ML methods.

Hub Design

Investigated the effect of the variation of viscosity of the aqueous glycerin solution and comparing the experimental results to a highly simplified model.

Wheel Center Design

I built a non-ballistic, kinematic model in python to simulate the scatter patterns produced by a ball colliding with a peg, where the initial position of the ball is dictated by some statistical distribution. I then validated the model mathematically.

Rocker Design

Proposed a novel method to measure the intensity profile of a single slit diffraction pattern, and validated the method via comparing it with theoretical predictions.
